Nourish Ingredients

Head of Design · Food-tech startup B2B

Nourish Ingredients creates animal-free fats through precision fermentation, one of the more technically ambitious companies in Australian food tech. I joined as Head of Design and rebuilt the brand from the ground up: identity system, tone of voice, product architecture, packaging, photography direction, website, sales automations, investor decks and internal systems. Working closely with the C-suite and marketing team, the challenge was making deep tech feel clear, desirable and commercially ready. The result was a brand that helped position Nourish Ingredients as a leader in novel food.

Who Gives A Crap

Design Lead · Toilet Paper Subscription B2C

I moved to Los Angeles to sell toilet paper. Who Gives A Crap relocated part of the team during a period of rapid growth across the US and Europe. Working as an integrated designer in a fast, scrappy environment, I worked across campaigns, limited editions, socials, impact communications and eDMs. I built the company’s first blog, Talking Crap, hired and directed a video team as the brand leaned heavily into paid social, and helped establish systems for rapid ideation and content production. The role sharpened my understanding of performance design: moving quickly, staying creatively unpredictable and tying output directly to commercial results.

AutoQuote

Solo build · Service business

Tradies don’t ignore leads because they don’t care. They miss them because they’re already working. AutoQuote is a quoting and lead qualification system designed for busy trades and service businesses. The product captures missed enquiries, automates customer intake, generates estimate ranges, and prepares qualified leads without disrupting on-site work. I designed the experience around speed, simplicity, and real-world trade behaviour, using lightweight automation and mobile-first workflows to remove admin friction and reduce lost opportunities. This case study explores the UX thinking, system design, and workflow architecture behind the product.

Minimetas

Solo build · NFTs & Web3

I’ve collected art since I was a teenager. Back in London, I was lost in the screen print scene around Pictures On Walls, chasing limited releases and arriving slightly too late to grab the now-famous Banksy editions. That feeling never left me: the drop, the scarcity and the culture around collecting something meaningful. When artists like Beeple and projects like CryptoPunks and Art Blocks started experimenting with blockchain, I immediately understood the appeal. During Covid lockdowns I started minting my own pixel art collection called Minimetas: handcrafted pixel characters released into the wild as a creative experiment in digital collecting and online communities.

Hey Neon

Solo build · Neon Maker

I love illustration, vintage signage and neon. While living in Los Angeles, I ended up next door to MONA, the Museum of Neon Art, and had the chance to study traditional glass bending and neon fabrication under a practising artist. It’s a surprisingly physical craft built around heat, timing and muscle memory, and one that’s disappearing quickly as LEDs replace traditional neon. When I moved back to Australia, I built a small studio at home called Hey Neon to keep the craft alive as an ongoing passion project.
